Head: This includes the skull, brain, eyes, ears, nose, and mouth.

Neck: Connects the head to the rest of the body and contains important structures like the throat and neck muscles.

Torso or Trunk: This is the main part of the body, consisting of the chest, abdomen, and back.

Upper Limbs: These are the arms and hands, including the shoulder, upper arm, forearm, wrist, and fingers.

Lower Limbs: These are the legs and feet, including the hip, thigh, lower leg, ankle, and toes.

Thorax: The area between the neck and abdomen, housing important organs like the heart and lungs.

Abdomen: The area between the thorax and pelvis, containing organs such as the stomach, liver, intestines, and kidneys.

Pelvis: The bony structure at the base of the spine, supporting the lower part of the body and containing the reproductive organs and bladder.

Back: The posterior aspect of the body, including the spine and associated muscles.

Muscles: These are found throughout the body and are responsible for movement and support.

Skeleton: The framework of bones that provides structure and protection for the body's organs.

Skin: The body's largest organ, providing a protective barrier and helping regulate temperature.
